Shot Map Design Alternatives

HockeyGameBot · Line Detail Page · Design Exploration · May 2026
Problem: The current shot map shows two half-rinks side by side — "Shots For" on the left, "Shots Against" on the right — both normalized to attack right. This creates two issues: (1) all maps look nearly identical regardless of line quality, because both FOR and AGAINST use the same orientation and similar dot densities; and (2) shots_against facing the same direction as shots_for is confusing — there's no visual cue that the opponent is shooting at your goalie. The four alternatives below explore solutions to both problems using real 2025–26 regular season data. Test lines: VGK Howden–Marner–Stone (good F line, 71.4% xGF), VAN DeBrusk–Kane–Pettersson (bad F line, 35.6% xGF), CBJ Severson–Werenski (good D pair, 69.2% xGF), CBJ Gudbranson–Provorov (bad D pair, 32.4% xGF).
Alt A — Baseline (Current)
current

Two half-rinks side by side, both attacking right. Goals filled red, non-goals as rings sized by xG. Problem demonstrated: a good line and a bad line look structurally similar — both show a cluster of shots in the attacking zone on both sides. The FOR/AGAINST distinction is only in the label.

Alt B — Mirrored Orientation
recommended

Shots For attack right (standard). Shots Against are mirrored — x coordinate flipped so the opponent attacks from the left side of their rink. A directional arrow label ("← attacking" vs "attacking →") reinforces the orientation. Solves: a bad defensive line should now show a mess of red shots crowding the mirrored net on the right side of the AGAINST panel — visually opposite to the FOR panel. The brain reads them as two teams going head-to-head, not the same team twice.

Alt C — Full Rink, Both Teams
ice level view

A single full-rink SVG showing both teams' shots at their actual ice positions. Blue/teal dots = shots for (attacking right half); red/orange dots = shots against (mirrored to left half). Goals are filled, shot-on-goals are rings. This is the true "ice level" view — the slot is at center, the boards are the edges, and you can see if this line was getting outshot in the middle or the perimeter. Solves: the full picture is visible at a glance. A bad pair shows the left half (against) crowded with red near the net while the right half (for) has sparse dots.

Alt D — xG Density Heat Map
pattern view

No individual dots. The attacking zone is divided into a ~12×8 cell grid (~8.3 ft × ~10.6 ft each). Each cell is colored by cumulative xG — white → light orange → deep red for shots_for (blue gradient for shots_against). Solves: a good line should show a hot slot cluster in FOR; a bad defensive pair should show a blue heat signature near the crease in AGAINST. This reveals shot selection patterns that individual dots obscure — is this line generating high-danger looks, or shooting from the perimeter?

Alt E — Smoothed KDE Density
gaussian kernel

Diverging KDE: each line's xG-weighted density minus the pooled average of all 4 test lines. Red = shooting more than average here. Blue = less. Cream = league average. Bandwidth ~25ft (wider than E1 → smoother contours). Cube-root color stretch so mid-range deviations are visible. This is what actually differentiates lines — because every line clusters shots near the slot, the absolute map looks the same. The deviation map shows whether a line is above or below typical shot selection at each location. Baseline: real 2025-26 regular season 5v5 shot distribution (87K shots), pre-computed with scipy gaussian_filter and loaded from league-baseline-kde.json. Falls back to avg of 4 test lines if the file is unavailable.
